# Key Findings

This summary highlights the most actionable patterns visible in the exercise data for the after-action review.

## 1. Alpha was the top-performing team overall, while Delta was the weakest

- Alpha posted the highest average accuracy at `83.18%`.
- Delta posted the lowest average accuracy at `73.06%`.

This suggests Alpha maintained the strongest overall decision quality across the exercise, while Delta may need the most attention in process, stress management, or team coordination.

## 2. Error rates peaked on Day 5

- Day 5 had the highest rate of flagged events at `47.92%`.
- Earlier days were lower:
  - Day 1: `34.04%`
  - Day 2: `36.17%`
  - Day 3: `42.86%`
  - Day 4: `31.48%`

This late-exercise spike may indicate accumulated fatigue, cognitive overload, or escalating scenario complexity.

## 3. Resource Allocation was the weakest cognitive domain

- Average accuracy by cognitive domain:
  - Resource Allocation: `76.20%`
  - Risk Assessment: `78.77%`
  - Pattern Recognition: `79.67%`
  - Situational Awareness: `79.89%`
  - Communication: `80.44%`

Resource Allocation appears to be the most consistent domain-level weakness and is a strong candidate for follow-on training emphasis.

## 4. Time-Critical decisions materially underperformed Deliberate decisions

- Deliberate decisions averaged `81.85%` accuracy.
- Time-Critical decisions averaged `72.90%` accuracy.
- That is a `10.94%` decrease relative to Deliberate decisions.

The gap becomes more concerning under high stress:

- High-stress (`stress >= 8`) Time-Critical decisions averaged `68.66%`
- High-stress Deliberate decisions averaged `79.88%`
- High-stress Collaborative decisions averaged `80.47%`

This points to decision tempo under pressure as one of the clearest performance vulnerabilities in the exercise.

## 5. Stress and accuracy showed a negative relationship

- The simple stress-to-accuracy correlation was `-0.2154`.

This is not an extreme correlation, but it is directionally meaningful: higher stress tended to coincide with lower decision accuracy.

## 6. Bravo and Delta showed the strongest improvement from Day 1 to Day 5

- Improvement in average accuracy from Day 1 to Day 5:
  - Delta: `+14.28`
  - Bravo: `+13.14`
  - Alpha: `+9.25`
  - Echo: `+8.03`
  - Charlie: `+6.44`

Although Delta finished with the lowest overall average accuracy, it showed the strongest day-over-day improvement across the exercise. That suggests meaningful adaptation even if absolute performance still lagged.

## 7. Crisis Response and Reconnaissance showed elevated error concentrations

- Scenario-level flagged event rates:
  - Crisis Response: `44.83%`
  - Reconnaissance: `42.22%`
  - Joint Operations: `41.86%`
  - Humanitarian Assistance: `37.84%`
  - Area Defense: `35.00%`
  - Convoy Security: `31.37%`

These scenarios appear to be the most error-prone contexts and may be useful focal points during the formal after-action review.

## Recommendations

1. Prioritize training interventions around Time-Critical decision-making under stress.
2. Use Resource Allocation as a targeted remediation domain across teams.
3. Review Day 5 conditions for fatigue, complexity, and workload accumulation.
4. Capture what drove Delta’s and Bravo’s improvement so those behaviors can be operationalized elsewhere.
5. Inspect Crisis Response and Reconnaissance event patterns for recurring overload or communication breakdown triggers.
